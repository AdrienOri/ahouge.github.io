Hi! My name is Adrien,

I am an astrophysicist in my 3rd (and final) year of PhD at the University of Exeter. My research focusses on the early stages of planet formation in discs around young stars. I spend my days coding or observing tiny dust particles with a bunch of different properties, and I feel so grateful to work in a domain that passionates me.
Before starting my PhD, I obtained my Master’s degree in Space Science at the University of Liège, in Belgium. I also had the opportunity to carry out a research project at the TRAPPIST-North Observatory, in Morocco — and participate in an Erasmus exchange with the University of Oulu, in Finland. Such international experiences in unexpected places made me hungry for adventures and the discovery of other cultures.

Apart from uncovering the mystery of the Universe, I enjoy a whole range of outdoor activities, from hiking and camping to climbing and ice swimming.
