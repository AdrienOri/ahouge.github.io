Science design

Overview

In addition to my coding adventures with tiny dust aggregates, I love to do graphic design. I believe that it is crucial, for us scientists, to find ways to share complex ideas and concepts using schematics simpler to understand. I recently started to use Adobe Illustrator and I have only scratched the surface of what this software is capable of, but I am already proud of what I have made. You will find below some designs I made for previous publications. They are all available in SVG if you want to use them for your own slides!

I also enjoy doing design for other people (in the field of astrophysics), it is is a great way to dive into new scientific questions, and I always find myself learning a LOT. So don't hesitate to get in touch!

This is my first design using Adobe Illustrator. It represents a protoplanetary disc with its dust particles, either dry or covered in ice if located outside of the water snowline (dark blue line). I made this schematic to show how an accretion outburst pushes the snowline outwards and drive the sublimation then re-condensation of water ice, and how that may affect the evolution of dust particles.

This schematic can be found in my first paper. It was even elected Astro Plot of the Week!

This schematic was made to visually represent the compact and porous dust models adopted in my first paper. We included a broad range of processes, with dust coagulation, fragmentation, sublimation, re-condensation, and compaction, so that a schematic is very helpful!

This schematic shows the structure of a Class 0/I protostar, outlining the chemical composition of icy dust grains at different scales. I built this schematic for the Master Thesis of Jenny Frediani, who worked with Leonardo Testi (University of Bologna) and Marta De Simone (ESO) to constrain the chemical conditions of IRAS 4A2 with ALMA. The goal is to show how the temperature gradient leads to increased molecular complexity in the ice mantles of dust grains, before sublimating in the so-called 'hot corino'.
